Mürrenbachfall

Mürrenbachfall is a high waterfall near the village Lauterbrunnen, Switzerland.The Mürrenbachfall is one of the highest waterfalls in Switzerland. With a single drop of appr 250 meter it belongs to the top 10 highest single drop waterfalls in Switzerland. It is especially imposing in spring after the snow melts and after thunder storms in summer. At other times, it is a mere trickle hidden between the cliffs, making the Staubbachfall the main attraction of the valley.

  • Cost: Free
  • Ages: Everyone

The Wengernaplbahn rail

Links the towns of Lauterbrunnen. It is located 2,061 metres above sea level and it has a length of 19,114 metres making it the longest cog railway in the world. The most travelled section of the route is between Lauterbrunnen and Wengen. The train provides transport for passengers and ensures a supply of goods for the car-free resort. It transports over 1,800,000 visitors every year.

  • Cost: Free with railway pass or CHF210($230)
  • Ages: Everyone
Make Reservation

Sphinx Observatory

The Sphinx Observatory is an astronomical observatory located above the Jungfraujoch in Switzerland. It is named after the Sphinx, a rocky summit on which it is located. At 3,571 m above mean sea level, it is one of the highest observatories in the world.

  • Cost: CHF 257(280)
  • Ages: Everyone
